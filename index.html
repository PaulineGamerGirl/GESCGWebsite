General Direction

I'm not spending council money on anything that doesn't directly protect or support a student. No paid general assemblies, no events for the sake of having events. The only things I will ever put real funds toward are emergency relief and research grants, because those are the moments where money is the difference between a student making it through the semester or not. Everything else on this platform costs zero pesos, which means none of it depends on whether we win a budget fight or raise enough donations. It is already feasible the day I take office.

This platform doesn't close once it's published. The suggestion tab means any student can add an idea at any point in the term, and they will get an actual reply, not silence. If they want to lead it themselves, we will help them do it.

I am running as a transwoman, and I am not going to soften that, perform it, or turn it into a punchline the way some campaigns turn minority issues into something cute or marketable. The students this platform is built for, disabled students, queer students, students who are hungry, students who are scared to file a grievance, deserve a council that takes them seriously, not one that turns them into a campaign aesthetic. That is the standard I am holding myself to.

I will spend nothing on what doesn't matter, and everything I have on the people who do.

Want to lead a project?

If you propose a new project and volunteer to lead it, here is how the process works:

  1. 1. Initial Review

    The executive team reviews your proposal for feasibility and alignment with college goals.

  2. 2. The Interview

    We'll reach out to schedule a casual chat to hear your vision and discuss the timeline.

  3. 3. Committee Assignment

    If approved, you become the Project Head! We will assign an experienced internal committee to handle the logistics and process documents (like Pre-Acts) so you can focus on the big picture.

  4. 4. Execution

    You lead the charge, and we provide the resources to back you up.
